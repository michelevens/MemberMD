# Employer Admin — Playbooks

You work in HR at a company that sponsors DPC memberships for employees at a MemberMD-using practice. Your job is to manage which of your employees are enrolled, keep the practice paid (sponsor invoices) for your share, and handle terminations / new hires cleanly.

You log into `/employer` — a portal scoped to your company's sponsored roster. You don't see anyone outside your employees, and you don't see clinical records (those are between the employees and the practice).

## Your work at a glance

- **Roster** — your sponsored employees + their enrollment status.
- **Invitations** — pending invites to employees who haven't enrolled yet.
- **Sponsor Invoices** — what your company owes the practice this month for sponsored memberships.
- **Reports** — usage and headcount summaries (no clinical data — that stays with the practice).

## The 4 jobs you do most

1. [First-week employer setup](./01-first-week-setup.md) — your contract, your roster upload, your first invoice
2. [Add new hires / remove terminations from sponsored roster](./02-manage-roster.md) — ongoing roster maintenance
3. [Pay a sponsor invoice](./03-pay-invoice.md) — monthly payment to the practice for sponsored seats
4. [Pull a utilization or headcount report](./04-pull-reports.md) — for HR / benefits-team reporting

## Important reminders

- **You can't see clinical data.** Per HIPAA, employee health information is between them and the practice. You see enrollment status, monthly seat count, and invoice amount — never diagnoses, visits, or labs.
- **Termination ≠ refund.** When you terminate someone, their membership goes inactive at the end of the period you've already paid for. You don't get retroactive refunds for the partial month (unless the practice's policy says otherwise — check your contract).
- **Mid-cycle joiners are prorated.** New hires added mid-month show up on next month's invoice with a prorated first-month charge. Don't worry about timing it perfectly.
- **The practice owns the relationship with the patient.** You're the payer; you're not in the clinical loop. If an employee asks you about their care, redirect them to the practice.
