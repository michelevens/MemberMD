# View your health records and past visits

> **For:** Patient · **Time:** 2–5 min · **Frequency:** As needed

## Trigger

You want to look back at what happened at your last visit, see what your provider noted, or check what meds you're on.

## Outcome

You've found what you needed in your record. If something looks wrong, you know how to flag it.

## Where

- [Health Records](/patient) — tab on your patient portal
- [Lab Results](/patient) — separate tab for labs (with trending)

## Steps

1. **Open Health Records.** You see a reverse-chronological list of your encounters (visits): date, provider, visit type, chief complaint.
2. **Click an encounter** to see the full note: subjective (your symptoms / history), objective (exam findings, vitals), assessment (the diagnosis), plan (what your provider did about it).
3. **For meds**, scroll to the Medications section of any encounter, or open the dedicated Medications view (link from the patient detail navigation).
4. **For labs**, switch to the Lab Results tab. Each result shows:
   - Test name + value + reference range.
   - Abnormal flag (green check / yellow warning / red alert).
   - Trend chart if you have multiple of the same test over time.
   - Provider's interpretation note (visible once your provider has reviewed and released).
5. **Download or print** an encounter or lab via the **"Download PDF"** button on any record.

## Watch-outs

- **Some sections might be hidden until your provider releases.** Lab results, especially, often show "Awaiting provider review" until your provider has read them and decided to release. This is a safety feature — you'll see the result with their interpretation, not raw and out-of-context.
- **You can read but not edit.** If something is factually wrong (wrong diagnosis, wrong medication, your name misspelled), use [Messages](./07-send-message.md) to flag it to your care team. They'll write an addendum — the original stays for the legal record, the correction is appended.
- **Records aren't shared with insurance.** Unless you explicitly authorize. The portal stores everything; it doesn't auto-submit to anyone.
- **Trends need history.** If you only have one lab of a given type, there's no trend chart yet. Run another in 3–6 months to see the trend.
- **Family member records.** If you're a primary subscriber with dependents, you can see their records by switching profiles in the top-right of the portal. Adult dependents (per practice policy) eventually get their own logins and you lose visibility — that's a HIPAA boundary, not a bug.
- **Old records from a previous EHR.** If your practice migrated from another system, only encounters generated within MemberMD show here. Ask your practice for legacy records separately if you need them.
- **Audit visibility.** Your practice can see when you opened a specific record (HIPAA logging on patients is intentional — same as them looking at yours). Not creepy, just standard.
