# Book, reschedule, or cancel an appointment

> **For:** Patient · **Time:** 3–5 min · **Frequency:** As needed

## Trigger

You want to book a visit, change one you already have, or cancel something you can't make.

## Outcome

Your calendar with the practice reflects what you actually need. The provider's calendar matches. You got a confirmation email + calendar invite.

## Where

- [Appointments](/patient) — tab on your patient portal

## Steps

### A. Booking

1. **Appointments → "+ Book Visit."**
2. **Pick visit type** — annual physical, follow-up, telehealth check-in, lab review, etc. Each shows duration + whether it's included in your plan or à-la-carte.
3. **Pick provider.** Usually your established provider; if you have multiple, pick by who matches the visit type.
4. **Pick a time.** A grid shows the provider's open slots. Pick one that works.
5. **Confirm.** Optional: add a note about what you want to discuss.
6. **Done.** You'll get:
   - Confirmation email.
   - Calendar invite (`.ics`) — drag into your calendar app.
   - Portal notification.

### B. Rescheduling

1. Find the appointment in the Appointments list.
2. **"Reschedule"** → pick a new time.
3. Confirm. New invite emailed; the old one is invalidated.

### C. Cancelling

1. Find the appointment.
2. **"Cancel"** → optional reason.
3. Confirm. You get a cancellation email.

## Watch-outs

- **Cancellation policies vary.** Some practices charge a no-show or late-cancel fee. Check your practice's policy; if you're cancelling within their cutoff window, expect a fee.
- **Telehealth visits don't auto-give you a link.** The "Join visit" button appears in your Appointments list when the time is close. Don't email the practice asking for a link — it doesn't exist until the moment.
- **State licensure.** Your provider is licensed in a specific state (or several). If you're traveling out of state at the time of the visit, the visit may need to be rescheduled. Tell your practice ahead.
- **Calendar invite is one-way.** When you import the `.ics`, you can add reminders / move it in your own calendar — but those changes don't sync back to the practice. If you reschedule, do it through the portal.
- **Waitlist.** If your preferred slot is taken, ask to join the waitlist. If another patient cancels, you'll get an offer for the slot (1-hour window to grab it).
- **First visits are usually longer.** Don't pick the 15-min follow-up slot for your first visit — pick the annual or new-patient slot. Practice may correct it but it's awkward.
- **In-person address.** The portal shows the address for in-person visits — double-check before driving. Some practices have multiple locations and the slot's address is what counts.
- **Family / dependents.** Book separately for each family member. The portal's Family selector at top-right switches the active patient context.
