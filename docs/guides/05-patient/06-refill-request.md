# Request a prescription refill

> **For:** Patient · **Time:** 2 min · **Frequency:** When you're running low

## Trigger

You're getting close to running out of a medication and you need a refill. (Common timing: 5–10 days of pills left.)

## Outcome

Your refill request reaches your provider; they review and either send the refill to your pharmacy, reach out to discuss, or decline (with a reason).

## Where

- [Health Records / Medications](/patient) — or the Messages tab
- Look for **"Request refill"** on the medication row

## Steps

1. **Open Health Records or your Medications list.** Find the medication you need refilled.
2. **Click "Request refill."** A short form appears:
   - **Confirm pharmacy** — should match the pharmacy you've been using. If it's wrong, update it now.
   - **Quantity needed** (optional) — say "30 days" or whatever your usual is.
   - **Optional note to provider** — "Same as last time" or "Could we increase the dose? Talked about this last visit" etc.
3. **Submit.** The request lands in your provider's inbox as a task / message.
4. **Wait.** Most refill responses come back within 1–2 business days. Don't refresh constantly; you'll get a portal notification + email when there's an update.

## Watch-outs

- **Don't wait until the last pill.** Build in 5–10 days of buffer. Refills sometimes need a conversation first, and pharmacies can be slow.
- **Controlled substances are different.** If your med is a controlled substance (Schedule II–V; e.g. Adderall, certain pain meds, certain anxiety meds), refills may require an in-person or telehealth visit first. The portal will tell you if a request can't be auto-fulfilled.
- **E-prescribing isn't fully live in MemberMD yet.** Your provider may send a paper Rx or fax instead of electronic. This is a temporary state until Surescripts ships (see [project_deferred_2026_05_04](../../../CLAUDE.md)). End result for you is the same — pharmacy fills your script.
- **Pharmacy on file.** If you changed pharmacies recently, update it in your Settings/Profile before requesting; otherwise the provider may send to the wrong place.
- **Refills counter on each med.** If a med shows "0 refills remaining" you can't get more without a new prescription — which usually means a check-in visit first.
- **Don't request what you don't need.** Hoarding meds isn't safe and disrupts your provider's clinical decisions on dosing.
- **If denied or modified**, your provider will message you with the reason. Sometimes they want to discuss labs, schedule a follow-up, or adjust the dose.
