# Send a secure message to your care team

> **For:** Patient · **Time:** 2–5 min · **Frequency:** As needed (typically a few times a month)

## Trigger

You have a question for your provider or the practice that isn't urgent enough to call but needs an answer.

## Outcome

Your message reaches the practice; they respond within their stated SLA (commonly 1 business day for routine, faster for urgent). The conversation is captured in your record.

## Where

- [Messages](/patient) — tab on your patient portal
- Or initiated from a specific visit / lab result with **"Message about this"** links

## Steps

1. **Open Messages.**
2. **Click "+ New conversation"** (or reply to an existing thread).
3. **Pick a category** (if your practice asks):
   - **Clinical question** — about symptoms, meds, results.
   - **Billing question** — about charges, plan, refunds.
   - **Appointment question** — scheduling, what to expect.
   - **Records / Admin** — requesting records, updating info.
4. **Subject line** — short, descriptive. Helps the practice route.
5. **Body** — be specific. "Symptom started Tuesday, gets worse with X, I've tried Y" is way more actionable than "feeling weird."
6. **Attach** files if relevant (a photo, a document) — encrypted at rest.
7. **Send.** The practice gets a notification. You'll get a portal alert + email when they reply.

## Watch-outs

- **Messages are NOT for emergencies.** If you have a medical emergency — call 911. Severe pain, chest pain, suicidal thoughts, signs of stroke, severe allergic reaction — call 911, not message us. The message inbox is not monitored 24/7.
- **Response time isn't instant.** Most replies come within 1 business day. Some practices guarantee 4 hours during business hours. Your practice's SLA should be in their welcome email; if you don't know, ask.
- **Be specific about meds and dosages.** "Lisinopril 10mg, started 6 months ago" is better than "my blood pressure pill."
- **Don't message your specific provider about urgent issues if they're off.** Send to the practice (general inbox); the on-duty provider will pick up. Your specific provider may be off for 3 days and not see your message.
- **2-way SMS isn't ready yet.** You can't reply via SMS to a notification. You have to log into the portal. Some practices may text you that you have a message, but you'll come back here to read/reply.
- **Photos are accepted but file size matters.** Phone-camera photos of rashes etc. work. Don't send 50MB raw images — they may fail to upload.
- **Conversations are searchable for you and the practice.** Old questions you asked 6 months ago are still there — useful for "what did we decide about this?" follow-ups.
- **Don't share PHI by email instead.** If you have a follow-up to a message thread, reply in the portal, not by emailing your provider directly. Email isn't HIPAA-safe; the portal is.
